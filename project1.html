Project Description

The project focuses on developing an efficient and automated image processing pipeline for segmentation of breast tumors using deep learning techniques. The primary architecture utilized for this task is the U-Net, a convolutional neural network (CNN) well-suited for biomedical image segmentation. The workflow is structured around key components of the U-Net, specifically encoder and decoder modules, to extract relevant features and reconstruct precise segmentation masks.

Outputs and Metrics

  • Precision: Measures the accuracy of tumor region identification in terms of positive predictive values.
  • F-measure: Represents the harmonic mean of precision and recall, ensuring a balance between the two.
  • Accuracy: Evaluates the overall correctness of tumor segmentation across the dataset.

Applications

This project has significant implications in the medical field, providing radiologists and clinicians with automated tools to detect and segment breast tumors with high accuracy. It supports early detection and treatment planning, improving patient outcomes.
